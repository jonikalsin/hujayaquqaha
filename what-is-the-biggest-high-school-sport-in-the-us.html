What is the biggest high school sport in the US?

Publish date: 2024-09-12

High school sports have long been a staple of American culture. For many young people, participating in sports is a rite of passage, a chance to develop physical and mental skills, and a way to create lifelong memories and friendships. But what is the biggest high school sport in the US? The answer is not as straightforward as you might think.

According to data from the National Federation of State High School Associations (NFHS), participation rates in high school sports have been steadily increasing over the past few decades. In the 2019-20 school year, more than 7.9 million students participated in high school sports across the country, an all-time high. This represents an increase of about 100,000 students from the previous year.

When it comes to the most popular high school sports, there are some clear winners. For boys, football remains the most popular sport, with more than 1 million participants in the 2019-20 school year. Basketball is the second most popular sport for boys, with about 540,000 participants, followed by track and field (nearly 510,000 participants), baseball (nearly 490,000 participants), and soccer (nearly 460,000 participants).

For girls, outdoor track and field is the most popular sport, with more than 494,000 participants in the 2019-20 school year. Volleyball is the second most popular sport for girls, with about 446,000 participants, followed by basketball (nearly 400,000 participants), soccer (nearly 390,000 participants), and softball (nearly 360,000 participants).

It's worth noting that participation rates vary by grade level and gender. For example, boys are slightly more likely to participate in high school sports than girls, with 49.3% of boys participating compared to 40.7% of girls in the 2019-20 school year. Participation rates also tend to decline as students get older, with the highest participation rates typically seen among freshmen and sophomores.

While the overall trend in high school sports participation has been positive, there are still significant disparities based on income, race, and geography. Students from lower-income families are less likely to participate in sports than their wealthier peers, and students of color are also less likely to participate than white students. In addition, participation rates can vary widely by state, with some states having much higher rates than others.

Despite these disparities, high school sports have many benefits for students. In addition to promoting physical fitness and teamwork, sports can help students develop important life skills such as leadership, perseverance, and time management. Sports can also promote inclusivity and gender equality, particularly for girls. Since the enactment of Title IX in 1972, which prohibits sex discrimination in education, participation rates in high school sports have grown significantly for girls.

However, the COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on high school sports participation. Many schools have had to cancel or postpone sports seasons due to safety concerns, and some families have opted to keep their children out of sports altogether. This has not only impacted the physical health of students but also their mental health and social connections.

In conclusion, the biggest high school sport in the US varies depending on gender and grade level. For boys, football remains the most popular sport, while outdoor track and field is the most popular sport for girls. While participation rates in high school sports have grown for girls since the enactment of Title IX, there are still significant disparities based on income, race, and geography. Despite these challenges, high school sports provide important opportunities for personal growth and can promote gender equality and inclusivity.
