Was Tammy Wynette Married To George Jones? Fans of the country singer are curious to know more about her personal life.

Tammy Wynette was an American country music singer, Actress, and author. She is regarded as one of the genre’s most influential and successful artists.

She contributed to bringing a female perspective to the male-dominated country music industry, which helped other women find representation in the genre.

Was Tammy Wynette Married To George Jones? Relationship Timeline

Yes, they were married. Tammy Wynette and George Jones were the first power couple in country music. Jones’ struggle with substance misuse caused the couple’s marriage to end in 1975.

They met in a Nashville recording studio in 1968 while Wynette married her second husband, Don Chapel, a part-time composer and hotel clerk.

She married Jones in February 1969, after her marriage to Chapel was dissolved. They quickly rose to prominence as Nashville’s answer to Elizabeth Taylor and Richard Burton.

Tamela Georgette Jones, Wynette and Jones’s only child, was born on October 5, 1970. Wynette and Jones had three children from previous marriages.

According to the New York Times, Wynette stated in her 1979 autobiography, Stand By Your Man, that Jones harassed her with a gun one night while drunk at their Florida House.

They began working together on music in 1971 when Jones joined Wynette’s record label, Epic Records.

In 1973, the singer of “I Didn’t Want to Play House” filed for divorce from Jones for the first time.

 A month later, they got back together, and Wynette told PEOPLE that it was just a ploy prescribed by Jones’ doctor to shock her hard-drinking husband into quitting drinking.

According to PEOPLE, Wynette filed for divorce from Jones for the second time in January 1975. “It’s all over. That’s it, “Wynette explained.

She married songwriter and producer George Richey in June 1978. Wynette died 20 years later, and the couple remained married.
