Sister Virginia’s Obituary: A Life of Service and Dedication

Introduction

Sister Virginia’s life was a testament to her unwavering commitment to serving others and her deep devotion to her faith. From her humble beginnings in Clarkston, Washington, to her role as the director of development for the School Sisters of Notre Dame Mankato Province, Sister Virginia’s journey was marked by selflessness and dedication. Join us as we explore the inspiring story of this remarkable woman and the impact she made on countless lives.

Early Life and Education

Born in Lewiston, Idaho in 1938, Sister Virginia spent her formative years in Clarkston, Washington. Raised in a nurturing environment that instilled in her the values of compassion, dedication, and a thirst for knowledge, she developed a strong foundation for her future endeavors. Graduating from Notre Dame Academy in Colton, Washington in 1956, Sister Virginia embarked on a path that would shape her life and the lives of those she would touch.

Admission into the School Sisters of Notre Dame

In 1958, Sister Virginia made a life-changing decision to join the School Sisters of Notre Dame in Mankato. This marked the beginning of her lifelong commitment to serving others and deepening her spiritual journey. Taking her first vows in 1958, she embraced a life of service, dedicating herself to the values of the congregation. Her decision to join the sisterhood was a testament to her unwavering faith and her desire to make a difference in the world.

Teaching and Administrative Career

Sister Virginia’s passion for education led her to embark on a fulfilling career as a teacher and administrator. From 1959 onwards, she taught at various schools in North Dakota, Minnesota, and Washington, leaving a lasting impact on the young minds she nurtured. Her dedication and expertise in the field made her a beloved figure among students, colleagues, and parents alike. Sister Virginia’s commitment to education was evident in her ability to inspire others to reach their full potential.

Director of Development


From 1989 to 1997, Sister Virginia assumed the role of Director of Development for the School Sisters of Notre Dame Mankato Province. In this capacity, she played a pivotal role in securing resources and support for the congregation’s mission. Her strategic vision and tireless efforts contributed to the growth and sustainability of the organization, ensuring its continued impact on the lives of many. Sister Virginia’s leadership and dedication were instrumental in furthering the mission of the School Sisters of Notre Dame.

Relocation and Return to Good Counsel

In 2014, Sister Virginia returned to Good Counsel in Mankato, a place that held a special significance in her heart. However, in 2022, she and the other School Sisters of Notre Dame relocated to Shakopee, embracing a new chapter in their lives. Despite the changes, Sister Virginia’s unwavering commitment to her faith and her community remained steadfast, leaving an indelible mark on all those she encountered. Her ability to adapt and continue her mission in new surroundings was a testament to her resilience and dedication.

Surviving Family Members

Sister Virginia’s life was enriched by the love and support of her family. Although she has now passed away, she leaves behind cherished memories and a legacy that will continue to inspire those who knew her. While her parents and siblings have all passed away before her, she is survived by her two nephews. They carry with them the memories of a beloved aunt who touched their lives with her kindness, wisdom, and unwavering faith. Sister Virginia’s family will undoubtedly find solace in the knowledge that she is now at peace, watching over them from above.

Funeral Arrangements

The funeral arrangements for Sister Virginia have been carefully planned to honor her life and provide an opportunity for loved ones to come together and pay their respects. These arrangements serve as a meaningful way to celebrate her legacy and find solace in the memories shared.

Funeral Mass and Visitation

A Funeral Mass will be held on Monday, January 8th, at 11:15 a.m. at Windermere Chapel in Shakopee. The Mass will be a solemn and reverent ceremony, offering a time for reflection, prayer, and remembrance. Prior to the Mass, there will be a visitation starting at 10 a.m., providing an opportunity for family, friends, and community members to offer their condolences and support to Sister Virginia’s loved ones. At 10:45 a.m., there will be a Sharing of Memories, where those who knew Sister Virginia can come forward and share their personal stories and experiences. This heartfelt tribute allows for the collective celebration of her life, highlighting the impact she had on the lives of others.

Burial in Mankato, Good Counsel Cemetery

Following the Funeral Mass, there will be a burial ceremony at the Good Counsel Cemetery in Mankato. This final act of farewell will take place at 2:15 p.m., providing a peaceful and serene setting for Sister Virginia’s eternal rest. The burial ceremony serves as a poignant moment of closure, allowing family members, friends, and the community to come together one last time to bid farewell to Sister Virginia. It is a time to honor her life, reflect on her contributions, and find comfort in the knowledge that she will forever be remembered and cherished.

Conclusion

Born in 1938 in Lewiston, Idaho, she spent her life in service to education and community.
