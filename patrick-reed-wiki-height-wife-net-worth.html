How did Patrick Reed start his Professional Career?

After winning the 2011 NCAA Championship, Patrick Reed started his professional career in 2011, when he was just 20 years old. Although he didn’t make the cut in his first PGA Tour event, the FedEx St. Jude Classic, he did participate in two Nationwide Tour competitions and earned about $5,000.

He competed in 12 PGA Tour events in 2012 and earned about $300,000. His best performance was a T-11 at the Frys.com Open. At the Wyndham Championship, he won his first PGA Tour championship. His third consecutive top-ten finish came at Sedgefield Country Club, where he also won.

After winning the WGC-Cadillac Championship in 2014 and taking home $1.53 million in prize money, Reed gained notoriety. With his victory, he joined the other five golfers who have achieved PGA Tour victory before the age of 24. As the youngest WGC champion, he rose to the top of the Official World Golf Ranking as a result of his triumph.

In 2015, he had a successful year, winning his fourth PGA Tour championship at the Hyundai Tournament of Champions. He also placed second at the Valspar Championship and third at the Hero World Challenge. He earned $1.98 million by winning the Masters Tournament in 2018 and the FedEx Cup in 2016 respectively.

In addition, Reed has triumphed at the Northern Trust, Liberty National Golf Club, two Ryder Cup competitions (in 2014 and 2016), three Presidential Cup competitions (in 2015, 2017, and 2019).

Awards And Achievements Of Patrick Reed

Among Patrick Reed’s many accolades and successes are his victories at the 2018 Masters Tournament, one World Golf Championship, and seven PGA Tour championships (the 2014 WGC-Cadillac Championship).

His accomplishments include being the youngest WGC champion and the first PGA golfer to have three victories prior to playing in a major. In addition, he is one of just four golfers under the age of 25 to have won four times on the PGA Tour.
